### H3: Gravity Fillers
- How they work (time-based, gravity-fed)
- Best for: thin, free-flowing liquids (water, light oils, thin chemicals)
- Limitation: cannot handle viscous or particulate products
- Viscosity range: up to ~100 cP

### H3: Overflow Fillers
- How they work (fill to visual level, recirculation)
- Best for: transparent containers requiring consistent fill appearance
- Handles thin to medium viscosity
- Key advantage: cosmetic fill level uniformity

### H3: Piston Fillers
- How they work (volumetric displacement)
- Best for: wide viscosity range including particulates
- Accuracy: +/-0.25% to +/-0.5% (servo-driven)
- Market dominance: 45.45% market share (2024)
- Limitation: more complex cleaning requirements

### H3: Pump Fillers
- Types: gear, lobe, peristaltic
- Versatility across viscosity range
- Accuracy trade-off: gear pumps ~1%, peristaltic 1-2%
- Best for: sensitive formulations, gentle handling requirements

### H3: Net Weigh Fillers
- How they work (scale-based measurement)
- Best for: expensive products, large volumes, weight-critical applications
- When to choose: high product cost or density variability

### H2: How Does Viscosity Determine Your Machine Choice?
- Viscosity as primary selection criterion
- cP value ranges with product examples (water to peanut butter)
- Machine recommendation by viscosity tier
- Counter-intuitive insight: machine construction can inadvertently change product viscosity
- Opinion: Get viscosity testing done before machine shopping

### H2: What Accuracy and Speed Can You Expect?
- Accuracy comparison with specific tolerances
- Speed ranges by machine type (30-600+ BPM)
- Trade-off analysis: speed vs accuracy vs cost
- Opinion: Don't over-specify speed - match to actual production needs

### H2: What Are the Most Common Selection Mistakes?
- Key mistakes from practitioner sources
- Nozzle sizing: 4-6mm smaller than bottle opening for air escape
- Container size vs machine capacity matching
- Ignoring maintenance and spare parts availability
- Price vs quality trade-off
- Opinion: Cheapest upfront is rarely cheapest long-term

### H2: Does Premium Equipment Actually Pay Off?
- ROI data: 18% less waste with piston systems vs pump
- Payback period: 12-18 months for premium equipment through material savings
- Labor cost reduction: up to 85% with full automation
- Machine lifespan: 10-15 years with proper maintenance
- Opinion: Calculate total cost of ownership, not just purchase price
