### H2: What Are the Three Stages of Potato Transportation?
- Stage 1: Pre-process (farm to facility)
- Stage 2: During-process (within facility)
- Stage 3: Post-process (facility to final destination)
- Opinion: This framework matters more than individual specs because it organizes all decisions

### H2: How Do Trucks, Trains, and Ships Move Potatoes?
- Trucks: Most common, 40-foot enclosed vans or reefers
- Rail: 10x more fuel-efficient than trucks (counter-intuitive insight)
- Ships: Refrigerated containers for international transport
- Counter-intuitive: Crisp potatoes travel farthest (1,000+ km), starch shortest (150 km)
- Opinion: Mode choice depends on product type, not just distance

### H2: What Temperature and Conditions Do Potatoes Need During Transport?
- Temperature varies by end use (not one-size-fits-all)
- Seed potatoes: 38-40F (3-4C)
- Table potatoes: 40-45F (4-7C)
- Processing potatoes: 45-55F (7-13C)
- Humidity: 95% optimal
- Ventilation: 10-16 air changes per hour
- Opinion: Temperature matters because it affects sugar content, which affects frying quality

### H2: What Challenges Do Potato Transporters Face?
- Cooling time: Truckers report 7+ hours to reach target temperature
- Cleanup: $120+ to clean bulk potato residue
- Cold chain integrity: Mechanical failures can compromise entire batches
- Remote areas: Tim O'Connor quote on transportation challenges
- Opinion: Real-world constraints explain why some loads arrive damaged despite "correct" specs

### H2: How Is Bruising Prevented During Transport?
- Economic stakes: $298M annual loss from bruising
- Maximum drop height: 40cm (16 inches)
- Stack limit: 8 tiers maximum
- Ideal harvest temperature: 12-18C (54-64F)
- Electronic potatoes (TuberLog) for damage detection
- Opinion: Prevention at harvest matters more than fixes during transport
