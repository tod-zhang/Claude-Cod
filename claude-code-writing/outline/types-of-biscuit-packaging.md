### H2: What Are the Main Types of Biscuit Packaging? (400-500 words)
Direct answer to core question with five primary formats:
- Flow wrap/pillow pack (most common, high-speed)
- Tray and sleeve (premium, fragile protection)
- Stand-up pouch (modern, resealable)
- Box/carton (traditional, gift positioning)
- Tin packaging (luxury, maximum protection)

**Opinion:** Flow wrap dominates for good reason - it balances speed, cost, and protection better than alternatives for standard biscuits.

### H3: Which Packaging Format Suits Each Biscuit Type?
Decision matrix matching:
- Plain/dry biscuits → flow wrap
- Cream-filled/sandwich → tray or metallized film
- Wafers/rolled cookies → rigid tray or canister
- Premium/gift → box or tin

### H2: What Materials Work Best for Biscuit Packaging? (300-400 words)
Material options with barrier properties:
- BOPP (standard, 6-month shelf life)
- Metallized BOPP (enhanced barrier, cream biscuits)
- Laminates (multi-layer for specific needs)
- Paper/cardboard (sustainability, requires coating)

**Opinion:** Metallized BOPP is worth the premium for any cream-filled product - the barrier properties prevent soggy disappointment.

### H2: How Do You Choose Between HFFS and VFFS Machines? (350-450 words)
Machine comparison for biscuits:
- HFFS: preferred for biscuits (up to 300 packs/min, gentle handling)
- VFFS: better for loose items (60-120 packs/min)
- Cost ranges ($4,500-$140,000 HFFS vs $3,000-$90,000 VFFS)
- Case study: Jacob White >200 cartons/min with quick changeovers

**Opinion:** Start with capacity matching - if your oven outputs 500/min, don't buy a 200/min packer.

### H3: What Maintenance Issues Should You Expect?
Operational reality:
- Crumbs accumulate around sensors - daily cleaning required
- Seal integrity issues with horizontal machines
- Integration with existing conveyors

### H2: What Are the Latest Trends in Biscuit Packaging? (250-350 words)
2024 sustainability developments:
- ITC Sunfeast 100% paper bag (Jan 2024)
- European PPWR regulation (all packaging recyclable by 2030)
- Consumer preference: 92% want eco-friendly options
- Compostable materials growing at 8.2% CAGR

**Opinion:** Paper-based packaging is no longer experimental - major brands are proving it works.

### Conclusion (80-120 words)
- Reinforce: Match biscuit type to format, material, and machine
