### H2: What Temperature Do You Need to Bend PVC Pipe?
- Direct answer: 210-275F is the ideal bending range
- Temperature zones explained:
  - Below 200F: Too cold, may crack
  - 210-275F: Ideal (soft but controllable)
  - 350F: Too soft (viscous, floppy)
  - 425F: Carbonizes (burns)
- Minor vs sharp bends: 225F for <30 degrees, 250-275F for >30 degrees
- **Opinion:** Higher temperature is NOT better - overheating causes more problems than underheating

### H2: What Tools and Materials Do You Need?
- Essential items list
- Heat sources: heat gun (350-400F setting), oven (for sand), or boiling water
- Kink prevention: sand, bending spring, or flexible metal conduit
- Safety gear: heat-resistant gloves, safety glasses, respirator recommended
- **Opinion:** A basic heat gun ($20-40) pays for itself after 2-3 projects vs buying fittings

### H2: How Do You Prevent PVC Pipe from Kinking When Bending?
- Direct answer: Fill the pipe with sand OR insert a bending spring
- Sand method details: Dry sand, cap one end, pour in, cap other end
- Spring method: Must be 1-2mm smaller than inner diameter for removal
- Alternative: Flexible metal conduit (greenfield)
- **Opinion:** Sand method gives most consistent results for DIY users - springs can be hard to remove

### H2: Step-by-Step: How to Bend PVC Pipe with a Heat Gun
- Preparation steps
- Heating technique: 3-4 inches away, constant rotation, even heating
- Bending process: Work slowly, hold position during cooling
- Cooling: Cold water bucket or wet cloth to set the bend
- Heating times by pipe size table:
  | Pipe Size | Heating Time |
  |-----------|--------------|
  | 1/2" | 2 minutes |
  | 3/4" | 4 minutes |
  | 1" | 5 minutes |
  | 1-1/4" | 6 minutes |

### H2: Alternative Methods: Heated Sand and Boiling Water
#### H3: Heated Sand Method
- Heat sand to 450F in oven
- Pour into capped pipe
- Bend when pipe softens
- Best for: Complex shapes, multiple bends

#### H3: Boiling Water Method (Use with Caution)
- Immerse pipe for ~5 minutes
- Limited to minor bends
- **Caution:** Mixed results reported - may not heat through uniformly

### H2: What Are the Safety Precautions for Heating PVC?
- **Golden Insight opener:** PVC cannot be extinguished once ignited - it burns without oxygen
- Fumes: Hydrogen chloride, vinyl chloride (carcinogenic if burned), carbon monoxide
- Work outdoors or in very well-ventilated area
- Required PPE list
- Fire safety: Keep extinguisher nearby, never use direct flame
- **Opinion:** If you're not comfortable working outdoors, use factory fittings instead

### H2: When Should You Use Fittings Instead of Bending?
- **Golden Insight:** "Use factory 45 and 90 degree bends wherever possible - it's not worth the time" (working electrician)
- Standard angles available: 22.5, 45, 90 degrees
- Use fittings when:
  - Pipe diameter >2 inches
  - Pressure rating matters (bent pipes lose strength)
  - You need precise, consistent angles
  - Angle >90 degrees
- **Critical Warning:** Bent PVC destroys molecular structure uniformity - never use for pressurized applications
- **Opinion:** For standard angles, factory fittings are faster and stronger

### H2: Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them
- Overheating (creates floppy, kinking pipe)
- Heating too quickly (uneven softening)
- No internal support for bends >30 degrees
- Trying to bend Schedule 80 like Schedule 40 (thicker walls need more time/heat)
- Bending pipes >2" diameter without specialized equipment
- Using bent pipe for pressurized applications
