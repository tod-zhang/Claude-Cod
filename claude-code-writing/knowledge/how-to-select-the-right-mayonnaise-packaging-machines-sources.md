### Main Findings

**Round 1 - Foundation & Technical:**

**Machine Types for Mayonnaise:**
1. **Piston Fillers** - Most common for mayonnaise; uses positive displacement; handles viscosities up to 100,000 cP; accuracy +/-0.2% to +/-0.5%
2. **Rotary Lobe Pump Fillers** - Gentle on product texture; suitable for shear-sensitive formulations; moderate viscosities; servo-driven versions available
3. **Servo-Driven Piston Fillers** - Premium option; "unparalleled accuracy and precision"; no reliance on external air supply; consistent throughout shift
4. **Peristaltic Pumps** - Easy to clean; cost-effective; limited for very high viscosity

**Packaging Format Options:**
- VFFS (Vertical Form Fill Seal) - sachets and bags; 5-60 bags/min
- Premade Pouch Machines - for stand-up pouches
- Jar/Bottle Filling Lines - most common for retail mayonnaise
- Stick Pack Machines - up to 400 packs/min for single-serve

**Round 2 - Data & Evidence:**

**Production Capacity Categories:**
| Scale | Daily Output | Recommended Equipment | Typical Speed |
|-------|--------------|----------------------|---------------|
| Small batch | <200 kg/day | Semi-automatic | 20-60 containers/min |
| Medium | 200-1,000 kg/day | Automatic single/double nozzle | 60-120 containers/min |
| Commercial | 1,000-4,000 kg/hr | Multi-head automatic | 120-250 containers/min |
| Industrial | >4,000 kg/hr | Rotary systems | 200+ containers/min |

**Accuracy Tolerance Standards:**
| System Type | Typical Tolerance |
|-------------|-------------------|
| High-precision weighing | +/-0.05% |
| Servo-driven volumetric | +/-0.25% to +/-0.5% |
| Standard piston filling | +/-0.5% to +/-1% |
| General food processing | +/-2% |

**Round 3 - User Perspectives & Alternatives:**

**7 Critical Mayonnaise Machine Mistakes (from Foodsure):**
1. Non-homogenized mixing vessels - Results in lumpy, separated mayonnaise
2. Incorrect viscosity control - Produces undermixed or overmixed batches
3. Poor temperature management - Damages emulsification
4. Improper ingredient timing - Adding oil too quickly causes separation
5. Wrong filling machines - Using liquid fillers causes clogs, leaks, uneven fills
6. Missing CIP systems - Manual cleaning risks bacterial growth
7. Non-scalable equipment - Forces costly equipment replacement

**11 Common Piston Filler Selection Mistakes (from Technopack):**
1. Ignoring product viscosity
2. Overlooking nozzle compatibility - "maintain 4-6mm clearance prevents splashing"
3. Disregarding container size
4. Neglecting filling mechanism type - foaming products need diving nozzles
5. Prioritizing low cost over quality
6. Ignoring machine speed
7. Forgetting maintenance and spare parts availability
8. Overlooking automation capabilities
9. Failing to assess production needs
10. Ignoring production line compatibility
11. Not seeking expert guidance

**Common Problems Encountered:**
- Inconsistent fill levels - due to viscosity variation, air bubbles, poor calibration
- Clogging issues - thick mayonnaise blocks pipes and nozzles
- Leakage and spillage - results in product wastage
- Particle precipitation - uneven filling with thinner sauces
- Worn-out parts - major cause of breakdowns

- Strict hygiene regulations in food production

**Servo vs Pneumatic - Key Differentiator:**
"No reliance on external air supply (unlike pneumatic fillers), ensuring fully consistent filling throughout the shift regardless of external conditions."
- Source: Shemesh Automation

**ROI Calculation Formula (from PLAN IT Packaging):**
- ROI = (Net benefit or loss from new equipment / Total new machine cost) x 100
- Payback Period = Total new machine cost / Overall periodic benefits from new machine
- Example: $400,000 equipment with $250,000 annual net benefit = 62.5% ROI, 1.6 year payback

**Heated Hopper Consideration:**
"Heated hoppers are particularly useful when filling highly viscous liquids that can become more manageable when warm, such as melted chocolate, caramel, or honey. The heated hopper helps keep the liquid at a consistent temperature."
- Source: https://technopackcorp.com/blogs/news/10-common-mistakes-to-avoid-when-choosing-a-piston-filler-machine-for-your-business

### Counter-Intuitive Findings

- **Common assumption:** Higher automation always means better ROI
- **Research shows:** Semi-automatic equipment (2-4 month ROI) can outperform full automation (6-12 month ROI) for small-medium operations - matching capacity to actual needs matters more than maximum capability

- **Common assumption:** Filling accuracy is standardized across machine types
- **Research shows:** Accuracy ranges from +/-0.05% (precision weighing) to +/-2% (general processing) - a 40x difference that directly impacts product giveaway costs

### Proposed Core Thesis

**PROPOSED THESIS:** Selecting the right mayonnaise packaging machine requires evaluating three dimensions simultaneously - production capacity needs, product viscosity characteristics, and total cost of ownership - rather than focusing on any single specification.

**Supported by:**
- Competitors focus on 1-2 dimensions only
