## Part 2: Target User Analysis

### User Type 1: The Complete Beginner

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Industry** | New facility managers, small business owners considering backup power, construction site managers, homeowners exploring energy independence |
| **Knowledge Level** | Novice (0-6 months exposure) |
| **Goals** | Understand power generation basics; learn about backup power options; make informed initial decisions about energy solutions |

**What They Already Know**:
- Basic awareness that power outages can be disruptive
- May have heard terms like "generator" or "solar power" without understanding differences
- General understanding that energy costs are rising

**What They Need to Learn**:
- What generators, hybrid systems, and energy storage do
- Basic types of power solutions and their differences
- Key specifications to consider (power output, fuel type, runtime)
- When to use which type of power solution

**Questions They Search For**:
- "What is a diesel generator?"
- "How does energy storage work?"
- "Generator vs battery backup"
- "Solar generator for home"
- "How to choose a generator"
- "What size generator do I need?"

**Typical Roles/Titles**:
- Small business owner evaluating backup power
- Residential homeowner considering energy independence
- New facility manager handling first power outage
- Construction site supervisor managing equipment needs

**Pain Points & Challenges**:
- Overwhelmed by technical specifications and unfamiliar terminology
- Fear of making expensive purchasing mistakes
- Uncertainty about power requirements for their application
- Difficulty comparing different power solution types and their trade-offs

**Content Preferences**:

| Aspect | Preference |
|--------|------------|
| **Reading Time** | 5-8 minutes; willing to invest time to learn basics |
| **Device** | Desktop/tablet (prefer larger screens for diagrams) |
| **Content Length** | 1,200-1,800 words with visual aids |
| **Format** | Step-by-step explanations, analogies, simple diagrams |

**Decision Factors**:
- Simplicity and ease of understanding
- Clear guidance on what solution fits their needs
- Transparent cost expectations
- Trust in the information source

**Writing Approach**:

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| **Tone** | Friendly, encouraging, patient |
| **Style** | Conversational; use analogies and real-world examples |
| **Complexity** | Simple sentences; define all jargon; avoid acronyms or explain them immediately |
| **Structure** | Start with "why it matters"; build concepts step-by-step |
| **Word Choice** | Everyday language; assume no prior knowledge |

**Audience-Specific Guidelines**:

✓ Use everyday analogies: "Think of a battery storage system like a giant phone battery for your home"
✓ Anticipate confusion and address it proactively: "This is where many people get confused, so let's slow down"
✓ Provide context before details: "Before we dive into types, let's understand why this matters"
✓ Use transitional phrases to guide: "Here's the good news..." / "The key takeaway is..."

✗ Don't assume any prior knowledge or write "As you probably know..."
✗ Never use unexplained acronyms (e.g., "Check the kVA rating" without defining it)
✗ Avoid chaining multiple concepts in one paragraph (voltage, frequency, and phase together)

### User Type 2: The Informed Non-Expert

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Industry** | Procurement managers, project managers, facility supervisors, site engineers in adjacent fields |
| **Knowledge Level** | Basic to Intermediate (6 months - 2 years exposure) |
| **Goals** | Select the right power solution for applications; communicate effectively with suppliers; evaluate quotes and specifications |

**What They Already Know**:
- General concepts of power generation and storage
- Basic terminology and common equipment types
- Awareness of major brands and suppliers
- Some hands-on experience with equipment selection

**What They Need to Learn**:
- Selection criteria and comparison frameworks
- How to match specifications to application requirements
- Common pitfalls in equipment selection and installation
- Best practices for procurement and maintenance planning

**Questions They Search For**:
- "How to choose a generator for construction site"
- "Diesel vs natural gas generator comparison"
- "Generator sizing guide"
- "Best backup power for telecom tower"
- "Hybrid power system benefits"
- "Energy storage system ROI"

**Typical Roles/Titles**:
- Procurement manager sourcing power equipment
- Project manager coordinating site power requirements
- Facilities supervisor managing building operations
- Site engineer in construction, telecom, or mining

**Pain Points & Challenges**:
- Balancing budget constraints with reliability requirements
- Evaluating competing quotes and specifications accurately
- Ensuring equipment compatibility with existing systems
- Justifying equipment choices to management or clients

**Content Preferences**:

| Aspect | Preference |
|--------|------------|
| **Reading Time** | 7-12 minutes; need actionable insights |
| **Device** | Desktop (50%), mobile (50%) - often on-site |
| **Content Length** | 1,800-2,500 words with comparison tables |
| **Format** | Decision frameworks, comparison charts, checklists |

**Decision Factors**:
- Practical applicability to their specific project
- Clear selection criteria and decision frameworks
- Real-world examples and case studies
- ROI and total cost of ownership considerations

**Writing Approach**:

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| **Tone** | Professional, practical, direct |
| **Style** | Action-oriented; focus on decision-making frameworks |
| **Complexity** | Moderate; can use industry terms with brief clarification |
| **Structure** | Problem-solution format; comparison tables; numbered lists |
| **Word Choice** | Industry-standard terms; occasional technical vocabulary |

**Audience-Specific Guidelines**:

✓ Provide decision frameworks: "Ask yourself these three questions before evaluating quotes..."
✓ Use comparison tables for different power solution types and their trade-offs
✓ Offer concrete selection criteria: "For continuous operation, look for generators rated for prime power, not standby"
✓ Include actionable takeaways: "Before requesting quotes, prepare these specifications..."

✗ Don't over-explain basics they already understand (e.g., what a generator is)
✗ Avoid deep technical details without practical relevance to their decision-making
✗ Never be wishy-washy - if "it depends," explain exactly what it depends on

### User Type 3: The Practitioner / Implementer

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Industry** | Electrical engineers, maintenance technicians, installation specialists, field service engineers |
| **Knowledge Level** | Intermediate to Advanced (2-5 years experience) |
| **Goals** | Install power systems correctly; troubleshoot problems; perform maintenance; optimize performance |

**What They Already Know**:
- Solid understanding of power generation fundamentals
- Hands-on experience with installation and maintenance
- Familiarity with common failure modes
- Technical vocabulary and industry standards

**What They Need to Learn**:
- Advanced troubleshooting techniques
- Proper installation and commissioning procedures
- Maintenance schedules and specifications
- New product features and integration methods

**Questions They Search For**:
- "How to parallel generators"
- "Generator maintenance checklist"
- "Battery storage system commissioning"
- "Hybrid system troubleshooting"
- "Generator load testing procedure"
- "Energy storage BMS configuration"

**Typical Roles/Titles**:
- Electrical engineer designing or commissioning power systems
- Field service technician maintaining generator fleets
- Installation specialist for hybrid power systems
- Maintenance supervisor managing equipment uptime

**Pain Points & Challenges**:
- Troubleshooting complex system integration issues in the field
- Meeting tight installation deadlines with limited resources
- Ensuring compliance with evolving electrical codes and standards
- Managing equipment warranty requirements during commissioning

**Content Preferences**:

| Aspect | Preference |
|--------|------------|
| **Reading Time** | 10-15 minutes; deep technical focus |
| **Device** | Mobile/tablet (60%) - often used in field, need quick reference |
| **Content Length** | 2,000-3,000 words with technical diagrams |
| **Format** | Step-by-step procedures, specifications, troubleshooting flowcharts |

**Decision Factors**:
- Technical accuracy and completeness
- Alignment with manufacturer specifications and industry standards
- Practical applicability to field conditions
- Clear troubleshooting guidance and safety warnings

**Writing Approach**:

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| **Tone** | Technical, precise, matter-of-fact |
| **Style** | Procedural; detailed step-by-step instructions |
| **Complexity** | High; assume technical competence; include specifications |
| **Structure** | Sequential steps; specifications; technical diagrams |
| **Word Choice** | Full technical vocabulary; use industry shorthand freely |

**Audience-Specific Guidelines**:

✓ Use numbered lists for sequential procedures with specific parameter values
✓ Bold critical specifications, safety warnings, and torque values
✓ Include units with all numerical values (kW, kVA, Hz, voltage, etc.)
✓ Reference applicable standards (IEEE, IEC, NEC) where relevant

✗ Don't explain basic electrical concepts they already know
✗ Avoid vague language - be specific about values, tolerances, and acceptance criteria
✗ Never skip safety warnings or assume "they know" safe practices

**Questions They Search For**:
- "Generator vs energy storage ROI"
- "Hybrid power system business case"

**Typical Roles/Titles**:
- Operations director managing facility infrastructure budgets
- Energy manager optimizing power reliability and costs
- Capital equipment buyer evaluating long-term investments
- Facilities executive balancing sustainability and financial targets

**Pain Points & Challenges**:
- Justifying significant capital investments in power infrastructure
- Balancing upfront costs with long-term operational savings
- Meeting corporate sustainability targets while maintaining reliability
- Navigating evolving energy regulations and incentive programs

**Content Preferences**:

| Aspect | Preference |
|--------|------------|
| **Reading Time** | 8-12 minutes; need strategic insights |
| **Device** | Desktop (70%) - reviewing during planning cycles |
| **Content Length** | 2,000-2,800 words with data visualizations |
| **Format** | Executive summaries, ROI calculations, trend analysis, case studies |

**Decision Factors**:
- Total cost of ownership over equipment lifecycle
- Risk mitigation and business continuity assurance
- Alignment with sustainability and ESG commitments
- Industry benchmarks and competitive positioning

**Writing Approach**:

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| **Tone** | Authoritative, strategic, confident |
| **Style** | Executive summary format; data-driven; outcome-focused |
| **Complexity** | Conceptually sophisticated but technically accessible |
| **Structure** | Lead with conclusions; support with evidence; use metrics |
| **Word Choice** | Business vocabulary; industry context; avoid deep technical details |

**Audience-Specific Guidelines**:

✓ Lead with business outcomes and strategic implications before technical details
✓ Use financial metrics (ROI, TCO, payback period) and industry benchmarks
✓ Provide data-driven evidence with citations to credible sources
✓ Frame technical choices in terms of business risk, opportunity, and competitive advantage

✗ Don't lead with technical specifications - start with business impact
✗ Avoid deep technical jargon without translating to business implications
✗ Never present options without clear recommendation based on business criteria

**Pain Points & Challenges**:

**Content Preferences**:

| Aspect | Preference |
|--------|------------|
| **Reading Time** | 15-25 minutes; comprehensive deep dives |
| **Device** | Desktop (90%) - research and reference material |
| **Content Length** | 3,000-5,000 words with technical depth |
| **Format** | Technical analysis, comparative studies, research citations, detailed diagrams |

**Decision Factors**:
- Technical rigor and depth of analysis
- Citations to peer-reviewed research and standards
- Novel insights or alternative perspectives
- Practical validation through real-world data or case studies

**Writing Approach**:

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| **Tone** | Peer-to-peer, analytical, nuanced |
| **Style** | Thorough analysis; acknowledge complexity and trade-offs |
| **Complexity** | Highest; assume full domain knowledge |
| **Structure** | Thesis-driven; comprehensive coverage; cite sources |
| **Word Choice** | Expert vocabulary; reference standards and research |

**Audience-Specific Guidelines**:

✓ Acknowledge complexity and explore nuanced trade-offs between approaches
✓ Reference specific standards (IEEE 1547, IEC 61850) and recent research papers
✓ Present alternative perspectives and debate points within the field
✓ Include technical depth: specific algorithms, control strategies, degradation curves

✗ Don't oversimplify complex technical relationships or skip edge cases
✗ Avoid making definitive claims without acknowledging limitations or alternatives
✗ Never cite outdated standards or ignore recent developments in the field
