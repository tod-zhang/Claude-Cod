## Part 2: Target User Analysis

### User Type 1: The Complete Beginner

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Industry** | New plant managers, maintenance trainees, engineering students, small business owners exploring automation |
| **Knowledge Level** | Novice (0-6 months exposure) |
| **Goals** | Understand what gearboxes do; learn basic terminology; make informed initial equipment decisions |

**What They Already Know**:
- Basic awareness that machines need power transmission
- May have heard terms like "gearbox" or "reducer" without understanding differences
- General mechanical common sense only

**What They Need to Learn**:
- What a gearbox/gear reducer does and why it's necessary
- Basic types of gearboxes and their differences
- Key specifications to consider (ratio, power, torque)
- When to use which type of transmission equipment

**Questions They Search For**:
- "What is a gearbox?"
- "Gear reducer explained"
- "How do gearmotors work?"
- "Types of industrial gearboxes"
- "Gearbox vs gear reducer difference"
- "Why use a speed reducer?"

**Typical Roles/Titles**:
- New plant managers evaluating equipment
- Maintenance trainees learning basic machinery
- Small business owners exploring automation options
- Engineering students in mechanical/industrial programs

**Pain Points & Challenges**:
- Overwhelmed by technical specifications and jargon
- Uncertainty about which gearbox type suits their needs
- Fear of making costly purchasing mistakes
- Difficulty communicating requirements to suppliers

**Content Preferences**:

| Aspect | Preference |
|--------|------------|
| **Reading Time** | 5-8 minutes |
| **Device** | Mobile & desktop (split evenly) |
| **Content Length** | 1,200-1,800 words |
| **Format** | Visual diagrams, short paragraphs, numbered lists |

**Decision Factors**:
- Clear explanations without overwhelming detail
- Real-world examples they can relate to
- Confidence that they understand the basics
- Knowing where to go for more advanced information

**Writing Approach**:

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| **Tone** | Friendly, encouraging, patient |
| **Style** | Conversational; use analogies and real-world examples |
| **Complexity** | Simple sentences; define all jargon; avoid acronyms or explain them immediately |
| **Structure** | Start with "why it matters"; build concepts step-by-step |
| **Word Choice** | Everyday language; assume no prior knowledge |

**Audience-Specific Guidelines**:

✓ Use everyday analogies: "Like the gears on a bicycle..."
✓ Anticipate confusion: "This is where many people get confused, so let's slow down."
✓ Provide context before details: "Before we dive into types, let's understand why this matters."
✓ Use transitional phrases: "Here's the good news..." / "The key takeaway is..."

✗ Never assume prior knowledge: Don't write "As you probably know..."
✗ Avoid unexplained acronyms: Never write "Check the RPM" without defining it first
✗ Don't chain multiple concepts: Don't introduce ratio, torque, and efficiency in one paragraph
✗ Avoid being condescending: Don't write "This is easy" or "Simply do X"

### Product Mention Guidelines

| Guideline | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| **Max Mentions** | 1-2 product references per article (solution-focused) |
| **Style** | Educational, not promotional. Product is THE SOLUTION, not the hero |
| **Placement** | Within relevant technical discussion, not intro/conclusion |
| **Tone** | "This can be addressed by..." NOT "Our product is..." |
| **Linking** | Link to product page only if genuinely helpful for reader |

**What Makes a Good Product Mention:**
- ✅ Appears where reader is looking for a solution
- ✅ Phrased as industry solution, not company advertisement
- ✅ Provides technical value (specs, capabilities)
- ✅ Reader would find it helpful even without buying

**What Makes a Bad Product Mention:**
- ❌ Feels inserted/forced into unrelated content
- ❌ Uses promotional language ("best", "leading", "superior")
- ❌ Appears in intro or conclusion (too promotional)
- ❌ Multiple mentions of same product
