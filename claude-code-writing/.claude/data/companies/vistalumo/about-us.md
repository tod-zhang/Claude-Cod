## Part 2: Target User Analysis

### User Type 1: The Entry-Level Buyer (入门新手)

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Industry** | New eyewear retailers, first-time importers, small business owners exploring eyewear market |
| **Knowledge Level** | Novice (0-6 months exposure to eyewear sourcing) |

- Eyewear is a profitable product category

**What They Need to Learn**:
- Difference between acetate, TR90, and injection-molded frames

- What certifications matter (CE, FDA, UV400)

- "Acetate vs plastic glasses"

- "MOQ for custom sunglasses"

- Small optical shop owners
- New e-commerce entrepreneurs
- First-time importers
- Startup eyewear brand founders

**Pain Points & Challenges**:
- Don't know which frame materials suit their market
- Confused by manufacturing terminology
- Worried about quality when buying from overseas
- Uncertain about minimum order quantities
- Fear of scams or unreliable suppliers

**Content Preferences**:

| Aspect | Preference |
|--------|-----------|
| **Reading Time** | 5-8 minutes |
| **Device** | Mobile-first (60%+ mobile) |
| **Content Length** | 800-1200 words |
| **Format** | Short paragraphs, bullet points, comparison tables |

**Writing Approach**:

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| **Tone** | Friendly, encouraging, educational |
| **Style** | Conversational; use comparisons and real examples |
| **Complexity** | Simple; explain all industry terms |
| **Structure** | Start with basics; build understanding step-by-step |

- Use comparisons: "Acetate is like premium plastic - natural, hypoallergenic, and more expensive than injection-molded alternatives"
- Anticipate confusion: "Many new buyers confuse TR90 with regular plastic..."

### Product Mention Guidelines

| Guideline | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| **Max Mentions** | 1-2 product references per article (solution-focused) |
| **Style** | Educational, not promotional. Product is THE SOLUTION, not the hero |
| **Placement** | Within relevant technical discussion, not intro/conclusion |
| **Tone** | "This can be addressed by..." NOT "Our product is..." |
| **Linking** | Link to product page only if genuinely helpful for reader |

**What Makes a Good Product Mention:**
- Appears where reader is looking for a solution
- Phrased as industry solution, not company advertisement
- Provides technical value (specs, capabilities)
- Reader would find it helpful even without buying

**What Makes a Bad Product Mention:**
- Feels inserted/forced into unrelated content
- Uses promotional language ("best", "leading", "superior")
- Appears in intro or conclusion (too promotional)
- Multiple mentions of same product
