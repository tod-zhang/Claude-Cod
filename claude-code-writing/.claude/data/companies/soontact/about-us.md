## Part 2: Target User Analysis

### User Type 1: The Complete Beginner

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Industry** | New production managers, small food business owners, entrepreneurs considering automation, students studying packaging |
| **Knowledge Level** | Novice (0-6 months exposure) |
| **Goals** | Understand packaging automation basics; evaluate if automation is right for their business; learn terminology |

**What They Already Know**:
- Products need to be packaged
- Manual packaging is labor-intensive
- Machines exist that can automate packaging
- Basic awareness of different package types (bags, boxes, pouches)

**What They Need to Learn**:
- Types of packaging machines and their applications
- Benefits and costs of automation
- Basic packaging terminology (VFFS, flow wrap, etc.)
- How to evaluate packaging solutions for their products

**Questions They Search For**:
- "What is a flow packaging machine?"
- "Automated packaging for small business"
- "Types of packaging machines"
- "How does VFFS machine work?"
- "Packaging automation benefits"
- "Sachet packaging machine explained"

**Typical Roles/Titles**:
- New production managers at small food companies
- Small business owners exploring automation
- Startup founders in food/consumer goods
- Operations coordinators with no packaging background

**Pain Points & Challenges**:
- Overwhelmed by technical terminology and machine options
- Uncertain if automation investment is justified for current production volume
- Difficulty understanding which machine type suits their specific product
- Lack of knowledge about space, utility, and infrastructure requirements

**Content Preferences**:

| Aspect | Preference |
|--------|------------|
| **Reading Time** | 5-8 minutes |
| **Device** | Mobile/tablet (60%+) |
| **Content Length** | 1,200-1,800 words |
| **Format** | Visual aids, step-by-step explanations, analogies |

**Decision Factors**:
- Clear explanation of benefits vs. costs
- Simple machine type comparisons
- Real-world product examples they recognize
- Guidance on when automation makes sense

**Writing Approach**:

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| **Tone** | Friendly, encouraging, patient |
| **Style** | Conversational; use analogies and real-world examples |
| **Complexity** | Simple sentences; define all jargon; avoid acronyms or explain them immediately |
| **Structure** | Start with "why it matters"; build concepts step-by-step |
| **Word Choice** | Everyday language; assume no prior knowledge |

**Audience-Specific Guidelines**:

✓ Use everyday analogies: "A flow wrapper works like gift-wrapping a present, but at high speed..."
✓ Show practical examples: "For example, the granola bars you see at the store..."
✓ Connect concepts to familiar products they use daily
✓ Celebrate understanding milestones

✗ Assume knowledge of packaging industry terms
✗ Use unexplained acronyms (VFFS, HFFS, OPP, etc.)
✗ Present too many machine types at once without clear categorization

### Product Mention Guidelines

| Guideline | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| **Max Mentions** | 1-2 product references per article (solution-focused) |
| **Style** | Educational, not promotional. Product is THE SOLUTION, not the hero |
| **Placement** | Within relevant technical discussion, not intro/conclusion |
| **Tone** | "This can be addressed by..." NOT "Our product is..." |
| **Linking** | Link to product page only if genuinely helpful for reader |

**What Makes a Good Product Mention:**
- ✅ Appears where reader is looking for a solution
- ✅ Phrased as industry solution, not company advertisement
- ✅ Provides technical value (specs, capabilities)
- ✅ Reader would find it helpful even without buying

**What Makes a Bad Product Mention:**
- ❌ Feels inserted/forced into unrelated content
- ❌ Uses promotional language ("best", "leading", "superior")
- ❌ Appears in intro or conclusion (too promotional)
- ❌ Multiple mentions of same product
