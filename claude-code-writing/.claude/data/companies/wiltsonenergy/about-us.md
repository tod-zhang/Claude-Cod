## Part 2: Target User Analysis

### User Type 1: The Complete Beginner

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Industry** | New procurement staff, facility managers exploring battery options, small business owners needing backup power |
| **Knowledge Level** | Novice (0-6 months exposure) |
| **Goals** | Understand battery basics; identify application needs; make informed purchasing decisions |

**What They Already Know**:
- Batteries store and provide electrical power
- Different battery types exist (lead-acid, lithium, etc.)
- Batteries need to be replaced periodically
- Basic awareness of charging requirements

**What They Need to Learn**:
- What LiFePO4 batteries are and why they matter
- Advantages over lead-acid batteries
- Basic battery terminology (capacity, voltage, C-rate)
- How to match batteries to applications
- Safety considerations

**Questions They Search For**:
- "What is LiFePO4 battery?"
- "Lithium vs lead acid battery difference"
- "Best battery for solar storage"
- "How long do lithium batteries last?"
- "26650 battery explained"
- "Low temperature battery options"

**Typical Roles/Titles**:
- New procurement staff at manufacturing companies
- Small business owners exploring backup power solutions
- Facility managers upgrading to lithium batteries
- DIY enthusiasts building solar systems

**Pain Points & Challenges**:
- Confusion about different lithium battery chemistries
- Uncertainty about compatibility with existing systems
- Fear of overpaying or choosing wrong specifications
- Concern about battery safety and lifespan

**Content Preferences**:

| Aspect | Preference |
|--------|-----------|
| **Reading Time** | 5-8 minutes |
| **Device** | Mobile-first (60%+ mobile) |
| **Content Length** | 800-1200 words |
| **Format** | Short paragraphs, bullet points, comparison charts |

**Decision Factors**:
- Clear explanation of battery benefits and limitations
- Visual comparison with familiar technologies (lead-acid)
- Simple guidance on application matching
- Confidence in safety and reliability

**Writing Approach**:

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| **Tone** | Friendly, encouraging, patient |
| **Style** | Conversational; use analogies and real-world examples |
| **Complexity** | Simple sentences; define all jargon; avoid acronyms or explain them immediately |
| **Structure** | Start with "why it matters"; build concepts step-by-step |
| **Word Choice** | Everyday language; assume no prior knowledge |

**Audience-Specific Guidelines**:

- Use everyday analogies: "Think of battery capacity like a fuel tank..."
- Anticipate confusion: "Many people confuse lithium-ion with lithium iron phosphate..."
- Provide context before details
- Don't assume any prior knowledge about battery chemistry
- Don't use unexplained acronyms (LFP, BMS, SOC)
- Don't chain multiple technical concepts together

**Questions They Search For**:

**Typical Roles/Titles**:

- Facility engineers managing power systems

**Pain Points & Challenges**:
- Matching battery specifications to application requirements
- Comparing offerings from different suppliers
- Understanding true total cost of ownership
- Ensuring compatibility with existing BMS and charging systems

**Content Preferences**:

| Aspect | Preference |
|--------|-----------|

| **Content Length** | 1200-1800 words |
| **Format** | Comparison tables, specification charts, decision frameworks |

**Decision Factors**:
- Practical selection criteria for different applications
- Specification comparisons with clear recommendations
- Temperature performance data
- Cycle life and warranty considerations

**Writing Approach**:

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| **Tone** | Professional, practical, direct |
| **Style** | Action-oriented; focus on decision-making frameworks |
| **Complexity** | Moderate; can use industry terms with brief clarification |
| **Structure** | Problem-solution format; comparison tables; numbered lists |
| **Word Choice** | Industry-standard terms; occasional technical vocabulary |

**Audience-Specific Guidelines**:

- Provide selection frameworks: "Consider these factors when choosing..."
- Use comparison tables for battery types and specifications
- Offer concrete criteria: "For temperatures below -20°C, choose..."
- Acknowledge trade-offs: "High-power cells offer faster discharge but reduced capacity"
- Don't over-explain basics they already understand
- Don't provide deep chemistry details without practical relevance

## Part 5: Author Personas

**Purpose:** Define consistent "voices" for content. Writers select a persona at the start of each article.

### Persona 1: 技术专家 / Technical Expert

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Best For** | Battery chemistry deep-dives, performance specifications, technical comparisons |
| **Role** | Senior Battery Systems Engineer |
| **Experience** | 15+ years in lithium battery design and testing |
| **Specialty** | LiFePO4 cell chemistry, low-temperature performance, battery pack design |
| **Bias** | Cycle life and safety matter more than energy density; real-world testing beats lab specs; thermal management is non-negotiable |
| **Voice Traits** | Data-driven, references test results, explains chemistry in practical terms |

**Signature Phrases:**
- "The datasheet says X, but in real applications..."
