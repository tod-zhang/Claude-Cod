## Part 2: Target User Analysis

### User Type 1: The Complete Beginner

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Industry** | Product designers new to manufacturing, startup founders, students, hobbyists exploring custom parts |
| **Knowledge Level** | Novice (0-6 months exposure) |
| **Goals** | Understand casting basics; evaluate if casting is right for their project; learn terminology |

**What They Already Know**:
- Metal parts are manufactured somehow
- General awareness that "casting" involves molten metal
- Basic understanding that custom parts can be made
- Some familiarity with common metals

**What They Need to Learn**:
- What metal casting is and how it works
- Different casting methods and when to use each
- Materials available and their properties
- Basic terminology (mold, pattern, pour, finishing)
- How to work with a foundry

**Questions They Search For**:
- "What is metal casting?"
- "How are metal parts made?"
- "Sand casting explained"
- "Types of metal casting"
- "Custom metal parts manufacturing"
- "Casting vs machining difference"

**Typical Roles/Titles**:
- Product designers new to manufacturing
- Startup founders developing prototypes
- Engineering students
- Hobbyists exploring custom metal parts

**Pain Points & Challenges**:
- Overwhelmed by foundry terminology (patterns, gates, risers)
- Uncertain which casting method fits their project
- Difficulty communicating requirements to foundries
- Fear of high costs or minimum order quantities

**Content Preferences**:

| Aspect | Preference |
|--------|------------|
| **Reading Time** | 5-8 minutes |
| **Device** | Mobile & desktop |
| **Content Length** | 800-1,200 words |
| **Format** | Visual diagrams, step-by-step explanations, comparison tables |

**Decision Factors**:
- Is casting the right manufacturing method for their project?
- Which casting process (sand vs investment) suits their needs?
- What information does a foundry need to provide a quote?
- How much will it cost and how long will it take?

**Writing Approach**:

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| **Tone** | Friendly, encouraging, patient |
| **Style** | Conversational; use analogies and real-world examples |
| **Complexity** | Simple sentences; define all jargon; avoid acronyms or explain them immediately |
| **Structure** | Start with "why it matters"; build concepts step-by-step |
| **Word Choice** | Everyday language; assume no prior knowledge |

**Audience-Specific Guidelines**:

✓ Use everyday analogies: "Think of sand casting like making a sandcastle—but in reverse..."
✓ Connect to familiar objects: "The engine block in your car was made this way..."
✓ Provide clear visual descriptions and celebrate understanding milestones
✓ Reassure about the process: "Working with a foundry might seem intimidating, but it's actually quite straightforward."

✗ Don't assume knowledge of manufacturing processes
✗ Don't use unexplained metallurgical terms or overwhelm with technical specifications
✗ Don't skip foundational concepts

### User Type 2: The Informed Non-Expert

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Industry** | Product engineers, procurement managers, design engineers with limited casting experience, project managers |
| **Knowledge Level** | Basic to Intermediate (6 months - 2 years exposure) |
| **Goals** | Select appropriate casting method; evaluate foundry capabilities; specify requirements; compare quotes |

**What They Already Know**:
- Basic casting concepts
- General material properties
- Project requirements and constraints
- Purchasing and vendor evaluation basics

**What They Need to Learn**:
- How to choose between casting methods
- Material selection criteria
- Design for manufacturability considerations
- What to include in casting specifications
- Vendor evaluation factors

**Questions They Search For**:
- "Sand casting vs investment casting comparison"
- "How to specify metal castings"
- "Casting tolerances guide"
- "Best casting method for [application]"
- "Metal casting RFQ template"
- "Foundry selection criteria"

**Typical Roles/Titles**:
- Product engineers with limited casting experience
- Procurement managers sourcing cast components
- Design engineers specifying custom parts
- Project managers coordinating manufacturing

**Pain Points & Challenges**:
- Choosing between sand casting and investment casting for specific applications
- Translating design requirements into foundry specifications
- Evaluating foundry capabilities and quality certifications
- Balancing cost, lead time, and quality requirements

**Content Preferences**:

| Aspect | Preference |
|--------|------------|
| **Reading Time** | 8-12 minutes |
| **Device** | Desktop (reference while working) |
| **Content Length** | 1,200-2,000 words |
| **Format** | Decision matrices, comparison tables, specification checklists, numbered frameworks |

**Decision Factors**:
- Which casting method provides the best tolerances for their application?
- What design features impact cost and manufacturability?
- How to evaluate foundry certifications and capabilities?
- What lead times and minimum order quantities to expect?

**Writing Approach**:

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| **Tone** | Professional, practical, direct |
| **Style** | Action-oriented; focus on decision-making frameworks |
| **Complexity** | Moderate; can use industry terms with brief clarification |
| **Structure** | Problem-solution format; comparison tables; numbered lists |
| **Word Choice** | Industry-standard terms; occasional technical vocabulary |

**Audience-Specific Guidelines**:

✓ Provide selection frameworks: "Consider these factors when choosing a casting method..."
✓ Use comparison tables for methods and materials
✓ Offer concrete criteria: "For parts under 50 lbs requiring ±0.005\" tolerance..."
✓ Include cost considerations and reference industry standards

✗ Don't over-explain basic manufacturing concepts
✗ Don't provide deep metallurgical details without practical relevance
✗ Don't overwhelm with options without clear recommendations

### User Type 3: The Practitioner / Implementer

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Industry** | Foundry technicians, pattern makers, quality inspectors, manufacturing engineers, machining specialists |
| **Knowledge Level** | Intermediate to Advanced (2-5 years experience) |
| **Goals** | Optimize casting quality; troubleshoot defects; improve processes; meet specifications |

**What They Already Know**:
- Casting process fundamentals
- Common defects and causes
- Quality inspection methods
- Material properties

**What They Need to Learn**:
- Advanced troubleshooting techniques
- Process optimization methods
- New technologies and techniques
- Specific material considerations
- Quality improvement strategies

**Questions They Search For**:
- "Casting defect troubleshooting"
- "Porosity prevention casting"
- "Sand casting gating design"
- "Investment casting wax patterns"
- "Casting shrinkage calculation"
- "Heat treatment castings"

**Typical Roles/Titles**:
- Foundry technicians operating casting equipment
- Pattern makers designing tooling
- Quality inspectors checking casting integrity
- Manufacturing engineers optimizing foundry processes

**Pain Points & Challenges**:
- Troubleshooting recurring casting defects (porosity, shrinkage, inclusions)
- Optimizing gating and riser systems for better yield
- Meeting tight tolerance requirements consistently
- Reducing scrap rates and improving first-pass quality

**Content Preferences**:

| Aspect | Preference |
|--------|------------|
| **Reading Time** | 10-15 minutes |
| **Device** | Desktop or tablet (shop floor reference) |
| **Content Length** | 1,500-2,500 words |
| **Format** | Procedural steps, troubleshooting flowcharts, specification tables, defect photos |

**Decision Factors**:
- What process adjustments will eliminate specific defects?
- Which standards (ASTM, ISO) apply to their application?
- How to optimize pouring temperature and gating design?
- What inspection methods ensure quality compliance?

**Writing Approach**:

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| **Tone** | Technical, precise, matter-of-fact |
| **Style** | Procedural; detailed step-by-step instructions |
| **Complexity** | High; assume technical competence; include specifications |
| **Structure** | Sequential steps; specifications; troubleshooting guides |
| **Word Choice** | Full technical vocabulary; use industry shorthand freely |

**Audience-Specific Guidelines**:

✓ Include specific values and tolerances: "Maintain pouring temperature at 1520-1560°C"
✓ Reference applicable standards (ASTM, ISO) throughout
✓ Provide troubleshooting guidance: "If shrinkage porosity occurs, increase riser size..."
✓ Include pro tips from foundry experience and safety warnings

✗ Don't explain basic foundry concepts
✗ Don't use vague language without specific criteria
✗ Don't skip safety considerations or proper protective equipment

| **Knowledge Level** | Conceptually advanced; operationally variable |
| **Goals** | Optimize supply chain costs; evaluate make vs. buy; assess vendor risks; plan capacity |

- Vendor relationship management
- Strategic sourcing

**What They Need to Learn**:
- Total cost of ownership for castings
- Supply chain risk factors
- Quality and delivery benchmarks
- Geographic sourcing considerations
- Sustainability factors

**Questions They Search For**:
- "Casting supplier evaluation"
- "China foundry sourcing risks"
- "Metal casting cost factors"
- "Supply chain localization casting"
- "Foundry quality certifications"
- "Casting lead time optimization"

**Typical Roles/Titles**:
- Manufacturing executives planning sourcing strategy
- Supply chain directors managing vendor relationships
- Operations managers optimizing production costs
- Business owners evaluating make-vs-buy decisions

**Pain Points & Challenges**:
- Balancing cost savings from offshore foundries with supply chain risks
- Evaluating total cost of ownership beyond piece price
- Managing quality consistency across multiple casting suppliers
- Mitigating lead time variability and logistics disruptions

**Content Preferences**:

| Aspect | Preference |
|--------|------------|
| **Reading Time** | 6-10 minutes |
| **Device** | Desktop & mobile |
| **Content Length** | 1,000-1,800 words |
| **Format** | Executive summaries, cost breakdowns, risk matrices, ROI calculators |

**Decision Factors**:
- What are the true total costs (tooling, shipping, quality issues)?
- How do they mitigate supply chain and quality risks?
- Which certifications and capabilities indicate a reliable foundry?
- What benchmarks exist for lead times and delivery performance?

**Writing Approach**:

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| **Tone** | Authoritative, strategic, confident |
| **Style** | Executive summary format; data-driven; outcome-focused |
| **Complexity** | Conceptually sophisticated but technically accessible |
| **Structure** | Lead with conclusions; support with evidence; use metrics |
| **Word Choice** | Business vocabulary; industry context; avoid deep technical details |

**Audience-Specific Guidelines**:

✓ Lead with business impact and bottom-line results
✓ Quantify cost factors: "Tooling represents 15-25% of first-order costs..."
✓ Address risk and mitigation strategies explicitly
✓ Connect to supply chain strategy and provide competitive context

✗ Don't dive into technical metallurgy without business relevance
✗ Don't ignore risk factors or present oversimplified solutions
✗ Don't use jargon without explaining strategic implications
