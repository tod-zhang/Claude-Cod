## Part 2: Target User Analysis

### User Type 1: The Complete Beginner

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Industry** | Product designers new to manufacturing, startup founders, students, hobbyists exploring custom parts |
| **Knowledge Level** | Novice (0-6 months exposure) |
| **Goals** | Understand casting basics; evaluate if casting is right for their project; learn terminology |

**What They Already Know**:
- Metal parts are manufactured somehow
- General awareness that "casting" involves molten metal
- Basic understanding that custom parts can be made
- Some familiarity with common metals

**What They Need to Learn**:
- What metal casting is and how it works
- Different casting methods and when to use each
- Materials available and their properties
- Basic terminology (mold, pattern, pour, finishing)
- How to work with a foundry

**Questions They Search For**:
- "What is metal casting?"
- "How are metal parts made?"
- "Sand casting explained"
- "Types of metal casting"
- "Custom metal parts manufacturing"
- "Casting vs machining difference"

**Typical Roles/Titles**:
- Product designers new to manufacturing
- Startup founders developing prototypes
- Engineering students
- Hobbyists exploring custom metal parts

**Pain Points & Challenges**:
- Overwhelmed by foundry terminology (patterns, gates, risers)
- Uncertain which casting method fits their project
- Difficulty communicating requirements to foundries
- Fear of high costs or minimum order quantities

**Content Preferences**:

| Aspect | Preference |
|--------|------------|
| **Reading Time** | 5-8 minutes |
| **Device** | Mobile & desktop |
| **Content Length** | 800-1,200 words |
| **Format** | Visual diagrams, step-by-step explanations, comparison tables |

**Decision Factors**:
- Is casting the right manufacturing method for their project?
- Which casting process (sand vs investment) suits their needs?
- What information does a foundry need to provide a quote?
- How much will it cost and how long will it take?

**Writing Approach**:

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| **Tone** | Friendly, encouraging, patient |
| **Style** | Conversational; use analogies and real-world examples |
| **Complexity** | Simple sentences; define all jargon; avoid acronyms or explain them immediately |
| **Structure** | Start with "why it matters"; build concepts step-by-step |
| **Word Choice** | Everyday language; assume no prior knowledge |

**Audience-Specific Guidelines**:

✓ Use everyday analogies: "Think of sand casting like making a sandcastle—but in reverse..."
✓ Connect to familiar objects: "The engine block in your car was made this way..."
✓ Provide clear visual descriptions and celebrate understanding milestones
✓ Reassure about the process: "Working with a foundry might seem intimidating, but it's actually quite straightforward."

✗ Don't assume knowledge of manufacturing processes
✗ Don't use unexplained metallurgical terms or overwhelm with technical specifications
✗ Don't skip foundational concepts

### Product Mention Guidelines

| Guideline | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| **Max Mentions** | 1-2 product references per article (solution-focused) |
| **Style** | Educational, not promotional. Product is THE SOLUTION, not the hero |
| **Placement** | Within relevant technical discussion, not intro/conclusion |
| **Tone** | "This can be addressed by..." NOT "Our product is..." |
| **Linking** | Link to product page only if genuinely helpful for reader |

**What Makes a Good Product Mention:**
- ✅ Appears where reader is looking for a solution
- ✅ Phrased as industry solution, not company advertisement
- ✅ Provides technical value (specs, capabilities)
- ✅ Reader would find it helpful even without buying

**What Makes a Bad Product Mention:**
- ❌ Feels inserted/forced into unrelated content
- ❌ Uses promotional language ("best", "leading", "superior")
- ❌ Appears in intro or conclusion (too promotional)
- ❌ Multiple mentions of same product
